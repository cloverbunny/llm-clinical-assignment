# Case 10: 51M — GI bleed, hemorrhagic shock

**Chief Complaint:** GI bleed, hemorrhagic shock

**HPI:** 51-year-old male admitted for massive upper GI bleed and hemorrhagic shock. Heavy drinker (12+ beers daily) with known cirrhosis (Child-Pugh B, MELD 18). This morning patient vomited large volume of bright red blood, approximately 1-2 liters. Presented to ED with hemodynamic instability: SBP 76, HR 138, RR 24. Two large-bore IVs placed, typed and crossed. Transfused 6 units pRBC, 4 units FFP, 2 units platelets. Octreotide started. GI consulted emergently; EGD shows variceal hemorrhage in distal esophagus, sclerotherapy not successful (patient off table for transfusion requirements). Planning repeat EGD after stabilization.

**PMH:** Cirrhosis (hepatitis C antibody positive, never treated), variceal hemorrhage x2 prior (last one 8 months ago, banded then), portal hypertension, thrombocytopenia (platelet baseline 65)

**PSH:** None

**Medications:** Propranolol 40 mg BID (non-selective beta blocker for portal HTN), Spironolactone 50 mg daily, Furosemide 40 mg daily

**Allergies:** NKDA

**Social History:** Single, unemployed, lives alone, active heavy alcohol use (12+ beers daily, denies interested in stopping), denies tobacco/drugs

**Physical Exam:** Pale, diaphoretic. Icteric sclera. Palmar erythema, spider angiomas on chest. Asterixis present. Abdomen distended, fluid wave appreciated. HR 102, BP 104/58 (on fluids), RR 24, temp 37.1°C. Lungs clear. Guaiac positive.

**Labs/Imaging:** Hgb 6.2 (post-transfusion), MCV 104, WBC 4.2, platelets 42, PT 18 seconds (INR 1.8), albumin 2.4, bilirubin 3.8, AST 1200, ALT 340. Cr 1.8 (suggesting hepatorenal syndrome). Lactate 4.2. Type and cross ongoing.

**Assessment:**
1. Massive upper GI bleed from esophageal varices
   - Known variceal hemorrhage x2 prior (last banded 8 months ago)
   - Current bleed ~1-2 L bright red blood
   - EGD confirms variceal hemorrhage, distal esophagus
   - Sclerotherapy failed
2. Hemorrhagic shock
   - SBP 76, HR 138 on presentation
   - Pale, diaphoretic
   - Lactate 4.2 (tissue hypoperfusion)
   - Massive transfusion protocol (6 pRBC, 4 FFP, 2 platelets)
3. Cirrhosis with portal hypertension
   - Hepatitis C antibody positive
   - Child-Pugh B, MELD 18
   - Clinical evidence: ascites, palmar erythema, spider angiomas, asterixis
4. Coagulopathy/synthetic liver dysfunction
   - INR 1.8, PT 18 seconds
   - Albumin 2.4 (reduced synthesis)
   - Platelets 42 (splenic sequestration)
5. Hepatic encephalopathy stage 1-2
   - Asterixis present
6. Hepatorenal syndrome
   - Cr 1.8 (markedly elevated)
   - Likely prerenal from hemorrhagic shock + cirrhosis
7. Hepatitis C (untreated)
   - HCV antibody positive
8. Active alcohol use disorder
   - 12+ beers daily, denies stopping

**Plan:**
1. Variceal hemorrhage management: Octreotide 50 mcg/hour infusion x 2-5 days
2. Repeat EGD: Once hemodynamically stable (goal SBP >90, Hgb >7)
3. Endoscopic therapy: Variceal band ligation (sclerotherapy failed)
4. Prophylactic antibiotics: Ceftriaxone 1 g IV daily
5. Hemorrhage control: Massive transfusion protocol, goal Hgb 7-9
6. Coagulopathy: FFP for INR <1.8, vitamin K 10 mg IV daily x 3 days
7. Hemodynamics: Monitor MAP, SBP; avoid over-transfusion
8. Renal/hepatorenal syndrome: Terlipressin + albumin if confirmed
9. Encephalopathy: Lactulose, rifaxomicin
10. Transplant evaluation: Hepatology consult for liver transplant candidacy
11. Alcohol use disorder: Psychiatry and addiction medicine consult
