# Case 6: 64M — Acute kidney injury, hyperkalemia

**Chief Complaint:** Acute kidney injury, hyperkalemia

**HPI:** 64-year-old male admitted for acute kidney injury with severe hyperkalemia. Patient reports 3 weeks of fatigue and malaise, progressive dyspnea on exertion. Known hypertension, recently started on ACE inhibitor and NSAID for joint pain by primary care. Presented to ED today after wife noted patient was confused and "acting strange." Labs showed K 6.8, Cr 4.2 (baseline ~1.0). EKG shows peaked T waves. Given calcium gluconate, insulin/dextrose, albuterol. Urine output has declined over past week—patient attributes this to urinary retention but denies dysuria.

**PMH:** Hypertension (20 years), osteoarthritis, former gout (not acute now), hyperlipidemia

**PSH:** TURP (2010)

**Medications:** Atenolol 50 mg daily, recently started Lisinopril 10 mg daily (2 weeks ago), Simvastatin 40 mg daily, Ibuprofen 600 mg TID (started 1 month ago for knee pain)

**Allergies:** Penicillin (rash)

**Social History:** Married, retired electrician, former smoker (quit 20 years ago), occasional beer, lives with wife

**Physical Exam:** Lethargic but arousable. Dry mucous membranes, poor skin turgor. No JVD. Lungs clear. No peripheral edema noted. Abdomen soft, no CVA tenderness. HR 58, BP 126/78, RR 20, temp 37°C. Foley catheter in place, draining dark urine.

**Labs/Imaging:** K 6.8, Cr 4.2, Hgb 10.1, Ca 8.2, PO4 6.5. Urine output 200 mL over 24 hours. Urinalysis with muddy brown casts, occasional RBC. BNP 850.

**Assessment:**
1. Acute kidney injury stage 3 (KDIGO)
   - Cr 4.2 (baseline 1.0), 4-fold increase
   - Oliguria, urinalysis with muddy brown casts consistent with ATN
   - Risk factors: ACE inhibitor + NSAID combination
2. Severe hyperkalemia (K 6.8)
   - EKG with peaked T waves
   - Risk for fatal arrhythmia
3. Medication-induced acute kidney injury
   - ACE inhibitor + NSAID combination causing hemodynamic injury
4. Volume depletion/pre-renal component
   - Dry mucous membranes, poor skin turgor
5. Anemia
6. Hyperphosphatemia
7. Altered mental status
8. Possible urinary retention

**Plan:**
1. Hyperkalemia management (urgent): STAT EKG, calcium gluconate, insulin/dextrose, albuterol
2. Medication adjustment: HOLD Lisinopril, HOLD Ibuprofen
3. Renal: Nephrology consult, monitor Cr Q4-6H
4. Bladder management: Assess for urinary retention, measure post-void residual
5. Hemodialysis: Prepare for dialysis if K >7.5 or EKG changes
6. Monitoring: Continuous cardiac monitoring, neuro checks Q2H
