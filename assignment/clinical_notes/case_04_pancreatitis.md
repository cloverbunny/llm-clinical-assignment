# Case 4: 67F — Acute pancreatitis, ARDS

**Chief Complaint:** Acute pancreatitis, ARDS

**HPI:** 67-year-old female with biliary pancreatitis. Presented to ED with severe epigastric pain radiating to back, onset 8 hours ago. Vomited 4 times. Known choledocholithiasis on prior imaging (refused intervention). Lipase 1240, amylase 850. Bedside ultrasound confirms gallstones. Developed respiratory distress within 2 hours of admission with O2 sats dropping to 82% despite supplemental O2. CXR shows diffuse bilateral infiltrates consistent with ARDS. Intubated, transferred to ICU.

**PMH:** Obesity (BMI 34), diabetes type 2, gallstone disease, hypertension, sleep apnea

**PSH:** Cholecystectomy attempted 1990 (complicated by bile duct injury, managed medically)

**Medications:** Metformin 500 mg BID, Atorvastatin 20 mg daily, Amlodipine 5 mg daily, Furosemide 20 mg daily (recently started for HTN control)

**Allergies:** Codeine (nausea), NSAIDs (gastritis)

**Social History:** Married, retired accountant, never smoked, rare alcohol, lives with husband

**Physical Exam:** Sedated, mechanically ventilated APRV mode, FiO2 0.65, PEEP 12. Tender abdomen, distended. Minimal bowel sounds. HR 104, BP 118/72, RR 22, temp 38.5°C. Lactate 2.8.

**Labs/Imaging:** Lipase 1850 (peak expected), AST 215, ALT 190, Cr 1.3 (baseline 1.0), calcium 7.2. Troponin negative. CT abdomen ordered.

**Assessment:**
1. Acute pancreatitis, biliary etiology
   - Choledocholithiasis confirmed
   - Lipase 1850, elevated liver enzymes
2. ARDS secondary to pancreatitis
   - Rapid onset of respiratory distress
   - Bilateral infiltrates on CXR
3. Acute kidney injury stage 1
4. Hypocalcemia
5. Ileus/acute abdomen
6. Type 2 diabetes

**Plan:**
1. Pancreatic/surgical: NPO, NG tube decompression, aggressive IV hydration
2. ERCP: Urgent ERCP for biliary decompression
3. ARDS management: Mechanical ventilation with lung-protective strategy
4. Renal: Monitor Cr, hold metformin
5. Electrolytes: Replicate calcium, monitor K, Mg
6. Nutrition: Assess when acute phase improving
