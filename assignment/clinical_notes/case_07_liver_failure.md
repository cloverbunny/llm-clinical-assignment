# Case 7: 55F — Acute liver failure, hepatic encephalopathy

**Chief Complaint:** Acute liver failure, hepatic encephalopathy

**HPI:** 55-year-old female with acute liver failure. Wife states patient was well 2 weeks ago. 10 days ago developed fatigue, decreased appetite, dark urine. 5 days ago patient became jaundiced. Yesterday developed confusion, asterixis noted. Wife also reports patient takes acetaminophen daily for arthralgia (approximately 4 grams per day for past 3 weeks, unaware of overdose risk). Prior alcohol use is "light" per patient's account but wife reports patient drinks 2-3 glasses of wine nightly. Presented to ED with INR 6.2, bilirubin 8.5, aminotransferases >1000. Referred for transplant evaluation.

**PMH:** Rheumatoid arthritis, osteoporosis, chronic hepatitis B (known for 10 years, never treated), depression

**PSH:** Knee arthroscopy (1995)

**Medications:** Methotrexate 15 mg weekly, Prednisone 10 mg daily, Sertraline 50 mg daily, Acetaminophen 4 grams daily (over-the-counter, self-prescribed), Calcium/Vitamin D supplements

**Allergies:** NKDA

**Social History:** Married, college educated, homemaker, 2-3 glasses wine nightly (per wife; patient reports "light" use), denies tobacco/drugs, lives with husband

**Physical Exam:** Jaundiced, confused, asterixis present. No signs of active bleeding. HR 94, BP 102/64, RR 22, temp 37.5°C. Abdomen soft, no organomegaly, no ascites appreciated. Lungs clear.

**Labs/Imaging:** INR 6.2, PT 48 seconds, AST 2140, ALT 1980, ALP 280, bilirubin 8.5, Cr 1.4, Alb 2.8, lactate 3.1. HBsAg positive, HBeAg positive, HBV DNA >7 log. Acetaminophen level 18 (therapeutic 10-20). Ammonia 180.

**Assessment:**
1. Acute liver failure
   - INR 6.2, bilirubin 8.5, aminotransferases >1000
   - Massive hepatocyte necrosis
2. Hepatic encephalopathy (West Haven Grade 2-3)
   - Confusion, asterixis
   - Ammonia 180 (elevated)
3. Etiology: Polyexposure hepatotoxicity
   - Acetaminophen overdose (4 grams daily x 3 weeks)
   - Alcohol use disorder (2-3 glasses wine nightly)
   - Chronic hepatitis B (HBsAg+, HBeAg+, HBV DNA >7 log)
   - Methotrexate (immunosuppressive)
4. Coagulopathy
5. Acute kidney injury/hepatorenal syndrome
6. Hepatic hypoalbuminemia
7. Alcohol use disorder
8. Depression

**Plan:**
1. Transplant evaluation: Urgent hepatology consult, assess for transplant candidacy
2. N-acetylcysteine: NAC loading dose then continue
3. Encephalopathy management: Lactulose Q2-4H, rifaxomicin 200 mg TID
4. Coagulopathy: FFP 2-4 units if INR >6.5, vitamin K 10 mg IV daily x 3 days
5. Renal: Monitor Cr, urine output; prepare for hepatorenal syndrome management
6. Hepatitis B: Start antiviral therapy post-transplant if candidacy confirmed
7. Psychiatric: Assess for suicidality, addiction medicine consult
8. Medication adjustments: HOLD acetaminophen, HOLD methotrexate
