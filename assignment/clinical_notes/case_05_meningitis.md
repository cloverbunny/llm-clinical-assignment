# Case 5: 31M — Meningitis, sepsis

**Chief Complaint:** Meningitis, sepsis

**HPI:** 31-year-old male with meningitis and sepsis. Presented to ED 4 hours ago with 2-day history of fever (max 105°F), severe headache, neck stiffness, and photophobia. Developed rash (petechial, non-blanching) 6 hours ago. Lumbar puncture performed: CSF protein 420, glucose 18 (serum glucose 95), WBC 8200 (90% PMN). Gram stain shows gram-negative diplococci (presumed meningococcemia). Started on ceftriaxone, vancomycin, and ampicillin in ED. Became hypotensive (SBP 78) and tachycardic, started on norepinephrine.

**PMH:** None. Immunizations current per patient.

**PSH:** None

**Medications:** None at baseline

**Allergies:** NKDA

**Social History:** Single, works in tech, lives in apartment, no tobacco/alcohol/drugs, recently returned from college dormitory visit

**Physical Exam:** Acutely ill, petechial rash on trunk/extremities. Neck stiffness, Kernig's sign positive. Alert but confused. HR 126, BP 94/56 on norepinephrine 0.08 mcg/kg/min, RR 24, temp 39.2°C. Normal lung exam, no cardiac murmur. Oliguria (0.2 mL/kg/hr).

**Labs/Imaging:** WBC 18.4, Hgb 11.8, Cr 1.6 (baseline ~1.0), lactate 5.2, PT/INR prolonged (1.8), platelets 45 (down from baseline 180 per wife). Blood cultures growing gram-negative diplococci.

**Assessment:**
1. Bacterial meningitis, meningococcal (Neisseria meningitidis)
   - Gram-negative diplococci on CSF gram stain
   - Classic presentation with petechial rash
   - CSF profile consistent with bacterial meningitis
2. Meningococcemia/sepsis
   - Petechial rash non-blanching
   - Shock requiring norepinephrine
   - Oliguria, altered mental status
   - Coagulopathy with thrombocytopenia
3. Disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC)
4. Acute kidney injury stage 2
5. Altered mental status
6. Septic shock

**Plan:**
1. Infectious disease: Continue ceftriaxone, vancomycin, ampicillin; blood cultures pending
2. Dexamethasone: 10 mg IV Q6H x 4 days if given early
3. Hemodynamics: Continue norepinephrine, aggressive fluid resuscitation
4. Coagulopathy/DIC: Monitor PT/INR, platelets, fibrinogen
5. Renal: Monitor Cr, urine output
6. CNS: Neurology consult, seizure prophylaxis if needed
7. Isolation: Droplet precautions for first 24 hours post-antibiotics
8. Close contacts: Recommend chemoprophylaxis
