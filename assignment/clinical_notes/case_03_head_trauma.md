# Case 3: 42M — Altered mental status, head trauma

**Chief Complaint:** Altered mental status, head trauma

**HPI:** 42-year-old male brought to ED by EMS after motorcycle accident without helmet. Hit by car at approximately 35 mph. Unresponsive at scene. GCS 5 on arrival (E1V1M3). Intubated immediately for airway protection. Head CT shows epidural hematoma 8 mm on right with 5 mm midline shift, multiple contusions. Neurosurgery consulted; deemed not surgical candidate at this time pending repeat imaging in 12 hours.

**PMH:** None documented. Social history suggests untreated hypertension (BP elevated since arrival).

**PSH:** None

**Medications:** None at baseline

**Allergies:** NKDA

**Social History:** Single, works in construction, regular marijuana use, heavy alcohol consumption (6-pack daily), lives with roommate

**Physical Exam:** Sedated, paralyzed for ventilator sync. Pupils 3mm, sluggish right. Bilateral lower extremity edema. HR 76, BP 156/92, RR 18 (set), temp 37.1°C. Right-sided weakness on withdrawal to pain.

**Labs/Imaging:** CBC/CMP pending. Alcohol level 0.18, THC positive. Urine drug screen positive for cocaine.

**Assessment:**
1. Severe traumatic brain injury with epidural hematoma
   - GCS 5 (E1V1M3) on arrival
   - Right-sided epidural 8 mm with 5 mm midline shift
   - Multiple brain contusions
2. Altered mental status from TBI
3. Airway compromise requiring intubation
4. Hypertension (likely from increased ICP)
5. Substance intoxication/use
6. Traumatic injuries to assess

**Plan:**
1. Neurosurgery: Repeat head CT in 12 hours, ICP monitoring and control
2. ICP management: Head of bed 30 degrees, avoid hypoxia and hypercarbia
3. Airway: Continue mechanical ventilation, reassess extubation post-imaging
4. Substance abuse: Social work and addiction medicine consult post-acute phase
5. Monitoring: Continuous cardiac monitoring, neuro checks Q1H
