# Case 9: 48F — DKA, pneumonia

**Chief Complaint:** DKA, pneumonia

**HPI:** 48-year-old female with diabetic ketoacidosis and pneumonia. Patient reports 1-week history of polyuria, polydipsia, malaise. Never diagnosed with diabetes until today. Presented to ED with Kussmaul respirations, altered mental status (GCS 12), fruity breath odor. Labs revealed pH 7.18, HCO3 8, glucose 456, betahydroxybutyrate elevated. CXR shows left lower lobe infiltrate. Cultures sent. Started on insulin infusion, aggressive IV hydration, and broad-spectrum antibiotics. Still on insulin gtt, target glucose 150-200.

**PMH:** None documented. Occasional hypertension. Recent upper respiratory infection.

**PSH:** Dilation and curettage (1998)

**Medications:** None at baseline (newly diagnosed diabetic)

**Allergies:** NKDA

**Social History:** Single, works in healthcare as administrative staff, lives with 2 roommates, recent URI exposure, no tobacco, occasional alcohol, no IV drugs

**Physical Exam:** Alert and oriented x2 now (improving with fluids/insulin). Lungs with crackles left base. Abdomen soft, mild epigastric tenderness. HR 102, BP 108/66, RR 22, temp 38.2°C. Urine positive for ketones.

**Labs/Imaging:** K 5.8 (will need repletion once >5.0 mEq/L with urine output), Cr 1.4, Cl 102, HCO3 8, glucose 320, anion gap 18, lactate 2.2, betahydroxybutyrate 4.2. WBC 12.4. Blood/sputum cultures pending.

**Assessment:**
1. Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA), severe
   - pH 7.18 (severe acidemia)
   - HCO3 8, anion gap 18
   - Glucose 456, betahydroxybutyrate elevated
   - Kussmaul respirations, fruity breath
   - First presentation of diabetes
2. Community-acquired pneumonia
   - CXR left lower lobe infiltrate
   - Crackles on auscultation
   - Fever 38.2°C, leukocytosis
   - Likely trigger for DKA presentation
3. Altered mental status
   - GCS 12 on arrival; improving with fluids/insulin
   - Likely from severe acidemia and hyperglycemia
4. Acute kidney injury stage 1
   - Cr 1.4 (likely new for young patient)
   - Prerenal from dehydration
5. Hyperkalemia (K 5.8)
   - Likely from acidosis
   - Will worsen initially with insulin, then improve with repletion
6. Newly diagnosed type 1 diabetes mellitus
   - First presentation with DKA (classic for type 1)
   - Will require lifelong insulin therapy
7. Volume depletion
   - Polyuria from hyperglycemia

**Plan:**
1. DKA management: Continue insulin infusion, target glucose 150-200
2. IV hydration: Aggressive saline resuscitation
3. Electrolytes: Monitor K, Cl, HCO3, pH Q2-4H; replete K once <5.5
4. Pneumonia: Continue broad-spectrum antibiotics, await culture results
5. Transition: Basal-bolus insulin once eating
6. Diabetes education: Carbohydrate counting, insulin injection technique
7. Renal: Monitor Cr, hold metformin
8. Monitoring: Continuous cardiac monitoring, neuro checks Q1H
9. Follow-up: Endocrinology consult, diabetes education mandatory
