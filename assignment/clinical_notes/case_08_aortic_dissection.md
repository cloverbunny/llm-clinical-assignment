# Case 8: 72M — Aortic dissection

**Chief Complaint:** Aortic dissection

**HPI:** 72-year-old male with acute type A aortic dissection. Presented to ED with sudden-onset, severe, tearing chest pain radiating to back. Known long-standing hypertension, poorly controlled. BP on arrival 198/110, HR 108. CTA chest shows Type A dissection starting at aortic root, extending into ascending aorta. Aortic root diameter 5.2 cm. Cardiothoracic surgery consulted emergently; patient consented to emergent surgical repair. Transferred to ICU post-operatively with new mechanical aortic valve and graft replacement.

**PMH:** Hypertension (35 years, poorly controlled), hyperlipidemia, type 2 diabetes, CAD (prior MI 2008), atrial fibrillation (paroxysmal)

**PSH:** PCI with stent (2008), left carotid endarterectomy (2012)

**Medications:** Metoprolol 100 mg BID, Lisinopril 40 mg daily, Amlodipine 10 mg daily, Atorvastatin 80 mg daily, Aspirin 81 mg daily, Apixaban 5 mg BID, Metformin 1000 mg daily, Nitroglycerin patch

**Allergies:** NKDA

**Social History:** Married, retired engineer, former smoker (quit 5 years ago, 50 pack-year history), minimal alcohol, lives with wife

**Physical Exam:** Post-op day 0, on propofol and fentanyl, sedated. Surgical incision intact, no signs of infection. Mediastinal chest tube in place draining serosanguinous fluid. HR 84, BP 132/68 on norepinephrine 0.04 mcg/kg/min, RR 18 (set), temp 36.8°C. Lungs clear. New systolic murmur at apex (from mechanical valve).

**Labs/Imaging:** Hgb 8.2 (post-op), Cr 1.3, Troponin I 2.4 (post-op, down from peak 3.2). INR 2.1 (on warfarin goal for mechanical valve). Lactate 1.8.

**Assessment:**
1. Type A aortic dissection, status post emergent surgical repair
   - Dissection from aortic root extending into ascending aorta
   - Aortic root 5.2 cm
   - Emergent surgical repair with Dacron graft placement
   - New mechanical aortic valve
2. Post-operative state (post-op day 0)
   - Hemodynamically supported on low-dose vasopressor
   - Troponin elevated (likely from intraoperative ischemia)
3. Mechanical aortic valve in place
   - Requires lifelong anticoagulation
   - New systolic murmur
4. Post-operative bleeding
   - Mediastinal chest tube draining serosanguinous fluid
   - Hgb 8.2 (post-operative anemia)
5. Acute kidney injury stage 1
   - Cr 1.3 (may be from perioperative hypotension)
6. Hypertension history
   - Long-standing, poorly controlled (likely predisposing to dissection)
7. Atrial fibrillation history
   - Paroxysmal; risk post-operatively

**Plan:**
1. Cardiac/Thoracic Surgery: Maintain close post-operative monitoring
2. Anticoagulation: Transition from apixaban to warfarin for mechanical aortic valve
3. Hemodynamics: Continue norepinephrine, target MAP 65-85 mmHg
4. Bleeding/transfusion: Monitor Hgb, transfuse if <7 g/dL
5. Respiratory: Continue mechanical ventilation, assess extubation readiness Q4H
6. Renal: Monitor Cr Q4H, maintain adequate hydration
7. Infection prophylaxis: Continue perioperative antibiotics
8. Discharge planning: Cardiac rehabilitation referral, warfarin monitoring clinic
