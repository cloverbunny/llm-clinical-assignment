# Case 2: 73F — Acute MI, cardiogenic shock

**Chief Complaint:** Acute MI, cardiogenic shock

**HPI:** 73-year-old female with chest pain that started at rest 6 hours ago, radiating to left arm and jaw. Associated with diaphoresis and nausea. Symptoms ongoing despite 325 mg aspirin given in ED. EKG shows ST elevation in II, III, aVF. Troponin I elevated at 2.8. Patient became hypotensive (SBP 82) and underwent emergent cardiac catheterization with stent placement to RCA. Now requiring norepinephrine for blood pressure support. Urine output minimal.

**PMH:** Hypertension (30 years), hyperlipidemia, chronic atrial fibrillation on anticoagulation, prior stroke (2015, mild residual weakness), type 2 diabetes

**PSH:** Pacemaker insertion (2018), hysterectomy (1992)

**Medications:** Metoprolol 50 mg BID, Lisinopril 20 mg daily, Atorvastatin 80 mg daily, Apixaban 5 mg BID, Metformin 500 mg BID, Aspirin 81 mg daily, Nitroglycerin patch

**Allergies:** NKDA

**Social History:** Widow, lives alone, former teacher, denies tobacco/alcohol/drugs

**Physical Exam:** Cool, clammy extremities. JVD 10 cm. Diffuse crackles in lung bases. Urine output 0.3 mL/kg/hr. HR 102, BP 92/58 on norepinephrine 0.1 mcg/kg/min, RR 24.

**Labs/Imaging:** Hgb 10.2, Cr 2.1 (baseline 1.0), BNP 1200, lactate 4.1. Troponin peak expected in AM.

**Assessment:**
1. Acute ST-elevation myocardial infarction (STEMI), inferior wall, RCA distribution
2. Cardiogenic shock post-MI
3. Acute systolic heart failure
4. Acute kidney injury stage 2
5. Chronic atrial fibrillation on anticoagulation
6. Type 2 diabetes
7. History of stroke with residual weakness

**Plan:**
1. Cardiac: Admit to ICU monitoring, continue dual antiplatelet therapy, manage vasopressor support
2. Hemodynamics: Continue norepinephrine, target MAP >65, consider inotropic support
3. Renal: Monitor Cr, urine output, hold metformin and ACE-I
4. Pulmonary: Monitor for pulmonary edema, cautious diuretics
5. Prophylaxis: DVT prophylaxis, GI prophylaxis
