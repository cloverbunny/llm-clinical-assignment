## Case 1: 58M — Respiratory failure, sepsis

**Chief Complaint:** Respiratory failure, sepsis

**HPI:** 58-year-old male admitted from ED with 3-day history of fever, cough, and progressive shortness of breath. Patient reports chills and night sweats. Initially seen in clinic 2 days ago for URI symptoms; discharged with supportive care. Fever spiked to 104.2°F yesterday. Wife reports patient became increasingly confused overnight. Presented to ED this AM with O2 sat 88% on RA. CXR showed bilateral infiltrates. Intubated for respiratory support.

**PMH:** COPD (moderate, on home O2), diabetes type 2 (20 years), hypertension, GERD, anxiety

**PSH:** Appendectomy (1995), left knee arthroscopy (2018)

**Medications:** Albuterol inhaler BID, Tiotropium daily, Metformin 1000 mg BID, Atorvastatin 40 mg daily, Lisinopril 20 mg daily, Omeprazole 20 mg daily, Sertraline 100 mg daily

**Allergies:** Penicillin (rash), Sulfa (rash)

**Social History:** Lives with wife, retired accountant, former smoker (quit 10 years ago, 40 pack-year history), occasional alcohol

**Physical Exam:** Sedated on propofol and fentanyl, mechanically ventilated FiO2 0.6, PEEP 8. HR 118, BP 142/88, RR 22, temp 38.9°C. Lungs with diffuse crackles bilaterally.

**Labs/Imaging:** WBC 15.2 (left shift), Cr 1.8 (baseline 1.1), lactate 3.2, procalcitonin 8.5. Blood cultures pending.
